# Seekho: Background

## What Seekho Is (What I Know)

Seekho is a short-form micro-learning platform founded in 2020. It has 25 million monthly active users, 100M+ downloads, and operates on a subscription model at Rs 200/month. Content is 3-5 minute videos in English, Hindi and Hinglish across ~40 categories including English Speaking, Career & Jobs, Business, Sarkari Kaam, Share Market, Exam Prep, and Self-Growth. All content comes from 250+ vetted creators.

The company has raised ~$42M total, including a $28M Series B led by Bessemer in August 2025. FY24 revenue was Rs 11.5 crore with 475% YoY growth. The team is about 64 people. The Series B is reportedly allocated across AI-powered personalization, new content formats, and international expansion.

## Why Seekho Works

Seekho's value comes from trust, curation, and format. YouTube has endless learning content, but for Tier 2/3 users the problem is finding the good stuff and knowing if it's accurate. Seekho solves that by being a trusted filter. In India, where bad advice on government procedures or financial products can have real consequences, that matters.

The format fits how Indians actually consume content. Short-form, vernacular, non-institutional. The tone feels like advice from someone you trust, not a lecture. And the curation saves time: users don't have to figure out which video is best. Seekho already made that call. That's what people pay for.

## The Problem As I See It

The surface problem is retention. Users watch a video, learn something in the moment, and forget it. There's no reinforcement, no spaced repetition, no practice loop. Content alone can't create lasting learning habits.

That's the gap the assignment is asking me to address.
